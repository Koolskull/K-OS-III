# Threat modeling

*This lesson is a stub. See `EDUCATION.md` for the project's curriculum and `docs/SEED_ISSUES.md` for the issue to flesh this out.*

This track teaches privacy as a practical discipline, not a vibe. The first move in any practical privacy work is threat modeling: deciding what you are protecting, from whom, at what cost, and accepting that no defense is total.

The track is honest about limits. Tor doesn't make you anonymous if you log into Gmail. Monero doesn't make you private if you withdraw to a KYC exchange the next day. End-to-end encryption doesn't help if your endpoint is compromised. Most "privacy tools" marketed to consumers buy modest improvements at best, and a few make things worse. The point of this track is to teach you to think in tradeoffs, not in slogans.
