# Funding Plan

This is a living document — not a business plan, not a set of promises, but a map of where the project can plausibly source support over the next 1–3 years. Items marked `[verify]` should be checked against the most current official page before applying; specifics change.

For the rules on what K-OS accepts money for and what it doesn't, see [`SPONSOR_POLICY.md`](./SPONSOR_POLICY.md).

## F. Unconventional and alternative funding

- **GitHub Sponsors** [`[verify]`](https://github.com/sponsors) for Koolskull and contributors. Already scaffolded in `.github/FUNDING.yml`. Low effort to set up.
- **Patreon / Ko-fi** for monthly support from users.
- **Merch** — shirts, pins, stickers, zines, cassettes/vinyl of in-OS music. Margin is small but brand-building is real.
- **Licensing** — Slimentologika, visual identity elements, aesthetic asset packs licensed to game developers, music producers, retro-hardware makers.
- **Commissioned work** — Koolskull or named contributors taking paid creative/technical work under the K-OS banner (music commissions, generative-art commissions, consulting on retro/pixel-correct interfaces for other projects).
- **Speaking and residencies** — conference honoraria (chiptune cons, FOSS cons, crypto/web3 creative events), artist residencies for the creator specifically.
- **Sponsored inclusion of open-source games or tools.** Realistic versions:
  - A studio or foundation that maintains an open-source game (SuperTux team, a demoscene group, a retro-style indie studio) pays K-OS to port their game to WASM and ship it in the default K-OS games bundle, increasing the game's visibility. Small sums, but real.
  - A hardware partner (Miyoo, Anbernic, a boutique cyberdeck maker) pays for first-party K-OS support and a co-branded build.
  - A FOSS game-preservation grant funds WASM porting of abandoned classic games to K-OS.
  - **Important caveat:** sponsorship that compromises editorial independence of the Reference, Reader Notes, or Education tracks **is not accepted.** See `SPONSOR_POLICY.md`.
- **Matching gift programs** — many tech companies match employee open-source donations. GitHub Sponsors integrates with many of these.
- **In-app tipping / pay-what-you-want** for specific premium assets — as long as the core OS remains free.
- **Bounty boards** — Gitcoin bounties, Algora — for specific issues the project lists. Good way to convert unallocated ecosystem funding into paid contributor work without applying for a full grant.
- **Retroactive public-goods rounds generally** — as more ecosystems adopt RetroPGF-style funding, tracking and applying is increasingly worthwhile for a project like this.

## G. Venture and investor routes (distinct from grants — note the tradeoffs)

- **Angel investors and crypto-native funds** interested in early-stage tools, especially those framed as creator infrastructure or crypto on-ramps with mainstream UX.
- **Honest note:** taking investment means the project is expected to generate financial returns. A project rooted in disintermediation, open-source licensing, and epistemic pluralism has to think carefully about what business model actually fits, and what governance structures protect the mission from being rewritten by incoming capital. Options include:
  - A foundation structure (mission-locked nonprofit)
  - A public-benefit corp with a mission-lock
  - Dual-entity arrangements (nonprofit + for-profit subsidiary, with the subsidiary licensing back to the nonprofit)
- **Recommendation:** consult a lawyer familiar with open-source commerce structures before raising. The choice of entity is hard to unwind once made.

## J. How this document is maintained

- **Keep the table data current.** When a grant program changes scope, fees, or eligibility, update the row.
- **Track applications.** When K-OS applies somewhere, log the application date in this file. When it's accepted or declined, log that too with one line on what was learned.
- **Add new sources** as they become known. The funding landscape changes faster than this document can stay perfectly current; that's fine — the discipline is to keep updating it.
